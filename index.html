Camel

  • Camels are mammals with long legs, a big-lipped snout and a humped back.
  • Camels' humps consist of stored fat, which they can metabolize when food and water is scarce.
  • Because of its characteristics, it is called as "ship of desert".

Chameleon

  • The Chameleon's eyes can rotate and focus separately on 180-degree arcs, so they can see two different objects at the same time.
  • Chameleons can also see both visible and ultraviolet light objects.
  • Chameleons can change their colour to protect themselves from predators.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
  • Pigeons have played a very important role in saving a countless number of lives throughout the history because of their homing skills as well as their pace.

Teddy Bear

  • A teddy bear is a stuffed toy in the form of a bear.
  • The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  • Teddy Day is always celebrated on February 10 across the world.

Panda

  • Pandas have great camouflage for their environment.
  • Adult panda bears can weigh as much as 45 kilos (100 pounds).
  • A panda will sleep about 10 hours a day.
